Filipino Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a beloved Filipino peanut stew made with tender oxtail, vegetables, and a rich peanut sauce. Served with shrimp paste (bagoong) on the side, it’s a hearty and flavorful dish perfect for family gatherings.

▶ Watch a tutorial

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs oxtail (or beef shank/tripe)
  • 1 small banana heart (sliced)
  • 1 bundle string beans (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1 small eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 bundle bok choy (pechay)
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter (smooth or chunky)
  • 1/4 cup ground toasted rice
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups water or beef broth
  • 2 tbsp annatto (achuete) oil for color
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bagoong (fermented shrimp paste), for serving

Peanut Sauce

Instructions

  1. Boil oxtail in water until tender (about 1.5 to 2 hours). Skim off any scum that rises to the top.
  2. In a separate pan, sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add the cooked oxtail and annatto oil. Stir to coat evenly.
  4. Pour in beef broth and bring to a simmer.
  5. Mix in peanut butter and ground rice. Stir well until the sauce thickens.
  6. Add the vegetables (banana heart, eggplant, string beans, and bok choy). Cook until tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with bagoong on the side and steamed rice.
